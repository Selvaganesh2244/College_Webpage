INFORMATION TECHNOLOGY





ABOUT

IT engineer is a professional in charge of the functionality of computer systems for an organisation. Those in this role work with computer software, hardware, networks and similar systems. They usually collaborate with other IT professionals to design and install equipment and programs and troubleshoot computer issues. An important part of the role of an IT engineer is to repair faulty computer systems and update technologies over time. Many IT engineers also perform management roles, supervising and guiding the assignments of entry-level IT professionals. In just ten years, our department has achieved many milestones. We now have 50 faculty members, each an expert in different areas of Information technology. Half of them have Ph.D. degrees, and many others are working towards them. We're proud that three faculty members have completed postdoctoral fellowships at international universities. Our faculty contribute through publications in journals, research articles, and conference presentations.

IT engineers often specialise in a particular field, such as:

  • Software development
  • Cloud computing
  • IT security and governance
  • Networking
  • Computer hardware
  • Systems architecture
  • Telecommunications
  • Business intelligence and analytics
  • Web design and development
  • Data science and machine learning

What does an IT engineer do?

  • Diagnose and resolve computer and technology-related issues
  • Identify which improvements and upgrades to make to computer systems
  • Manage projects and coordinate with other IT employees
  • Document and report solutions to clients, managers and other stakeholders
  • Work with clients to identify their technological and software needs
  • Design, configure and test custom computer systems for clients
  • Order supplies and equipment as necessary
  • Conduct research and coding to develop new software
